Especially in the wake of COVID, mental health has taken a serious toll on people across the world.With the insurmountable stress accumulating from quarantine, which can come from being laid off or dealing with an abusive family, more people are struggling than ever before.

Depression vs Suicide

Depression and suicide might seem similar, but they have very specific differences that have big implications on how to support and treat a patient. Depression, the root of suicide, is widely considered to be a key risk factor. Suicide is much more severe, and a lot more support is needed to treat a patient. Those who struggle often open up to their friends over text and send messages; however, these friends are often in the dark in terms of trying to understand their friend's mental state.

Friends and Family

People facing depression typically tend to resort to their friends first when seeking assistance. Friends are people that they can trust and are comfortable with allowing for a safe environment. Since all interactions are happening online, understanding your friend's mental health through interactions, so understanding their phrases and texts is the best way to understand their mental state.

Early Detection

It is very important that messages hinting at suicidal intentions are recognized right away. If noticed early, proper assistance can be provided in order to help those dealing with major depression. Especially due to COVID since hospitals and doctors are overloaded, people can't get the support that they need. Thus, having early detection online is the best way to help these people.

Our Process

We are a one stop shop for detecting suicidal and depressive tendencies and providing personalized support for those struggling with mental health. We are powered by AI/ML, NLP, and other algorithms to provide tailored diagnoses and support.

Primary Diagnosis

Using our basic NLP model, a friend can paste or write out text, and will be able to see if their friend is suffering depression or is on the suicidal side.                           

Progression Model

We also have a progression model which classifies the patient into one of 6 different cateogries based on Jessie Bering's classification which gives a more specific diagnosis for the patient.

Locating Therapists

The friend will be able to visit the map embedded within the website to see pairings with local therapists and create an effective support system for the struggling person.          

Getting Support

Friends and patients can consult qualified therapists on valuable questions they may not know the answer to through our forum feature. We hope to build an inclusive community where everyone can feel safe.

Frequently Asked Questions

Don't people know if someone is suicidal?

It might be possible to tell that someone is upset or even depressed, but it is incredibly difficult to determine if someone has suicidal tendancies as a person. It is possible to tell based on their messages and their phrases, which means using our algorithm is the best way to distinctively tell if someone is suicidal or not.

Who is this site specifically meant for?

This site is mainly meant for friends of those who are going through depression so they can accurately see specifically what their friend is dealing with. Additionally, therapists and doctors can also use our algorithm to better understand their patients especially when their phrases are hard to distinguish and cryptic.

How do I navigate through the site?

To use this site, you either directly type in a post or message from your friend. You will then receive an prediction on whether your friend is just suffering from depression or if they are suicidal. You will then go to the map and will be able to find local therapists near you. For additional support, go to the forum to ask for help or support.

Why is our site better than existing models?

Unlike current existing models, which send an automated robot to help interact with the depressed person, ours is a comprehensive step by step process that will involve an experienced properly trained therapist and will eventually root back to the friend. This is important because the person that is depressed is much more likely to be more receptive to advice from their friends and real human beings rather than automated messages.
